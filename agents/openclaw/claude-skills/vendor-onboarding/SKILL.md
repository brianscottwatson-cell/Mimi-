# Vendor Onboarding Skill

Onboard, enable, and activate vendor and channel partners through structured compliance, integration, and enablement workflows.

---

## Why

Vendor and partner relationships are force multipliers — when they work. Most don't, because onboarding is treated as paperwork instead of enablement. A vendor who completes compliance forms but never launches a co-marketing campaign is a partner in name only. This skill exists to build onboarding workflows that move partners from "signed agreement" to "generating joint revenue" as fast as possible.

**Core belief:** Vendor onboarding succeeds when the partner can independently sell, deliver, and support without hand-holding within 90 days.

### Inputs
- Partner type (reseller, referral, technology, co-sell, white-label)
- Partner tier (strategic, growth, emerging)

- **Co-marketing playbook** — MDF process, campaign templates, approval workflow

## Rules

1. **Compliance before enablement.** Legal, security, and contractual requirements must be cleared before investing in enablement. No exceptions.
2. **Integration before co-selling.** If the partner needs a technical integration, it must be tested and verified before joint GTM begins.
3. **Mutual commitment.** Onboarding plans must include commitments from both sides. If the partner won't invest time, deprioritize.
4. **SLAs on everything.** Every onboarding step has an owner and a deadline. Open-ended timelines kill partnerships.
5. **Enablement is not a PDF.** Partners need practice, not just documentation. Include live training, shadowing, or sandbox access.
6. **MDF is earned, not entitled.** Marketing development funds are tied to measurable outcomes and co-investment, not partner tier alone.
7. **Quarterly business reviews.** Every active partner gets a structured QBR within 90 days of activation.
8. **Single point of contact.** Each partner gets one internal owner (Partner Manager) who coordinates all internal resources.
9. **Sunset inactive partners.** If a partner hasn't generated pipeline or revenue in 180 days, trigger a re-engagement or offboarding workflow.

## Workflows

### Workflow 1: Full Partner Onboarding

End-to-end onboarding from signed agreement to first joint deal.

1. **Intake** (Pax)
   - Collect partner profile: company info, team contacts, capabilities, target market
   - Assign Partner Manager (single point of contact)
   - Create onboarding project in PM tool with milestones and SLAs
   - Send welcome packet with timeline and expectations

2. **Compliance & Legal** (Week 1-2)
   - [ ] Partner agreement executed
   - [ ] NDA in place
   - [ ] Insurance/liability verification
   - [ ] Security questionnaire completed
   - [ ] Data processing agreement (if applicable)
   - [ ] Tax/banking information collected
   - **Gate:** All compliance items complete before proceeding

3. **Technical Integration** (Week 2-4) (Dev)
   - [ ] API credentials provisioned
   - [ ] Sandbox/staging environment access granted
   - [ ] Integration developed and tested
   - [ ] Data mapping validated
   - [ ] SSO configured (if applicable)
   - [ ] Billing/invoicing integration tested
   - **Gate:** Integration passes acceptance testing

4. **Systems Access** (Week 2-3)
   - [ ] PRM portal access provisioned
   - [ ] Deal registration system enabled
   - [ ] Co-branded asset library access
   - [ ] Support ticketing access
   - [ ] Communication channels set up (Slack/Teams)

5. **Sales Enablement** (Week 3-5) (Cora + Vale)
   - [ ] Joint value proposition defined
   - [ ] Sales deck customized for partner's audience
   - [ ] Battle cards created (vs. competition)
   - [ ] Demo script/sandbox walkthrough
   - [ ] Objection handling guide
   - [ ] Pricing and quoting guide
   - [ ] Live training session (recorded for future reference)

6. **Co-Marketing Setup** (Week 4-6) (Mia + Finn)
   - [ ] MDF allocation and approval process documented
   - [ ] Co-branded content templates created
   - [ ] Joint campaign plan drafted (first 90 days)
   - [ ] Event co-sponsorship opportunities identified
   - [ ] Social media cross-promotion plan
   - [ ] Case study / success story plan

7. **Activation** (Week 6-8)
   - [ ] First joint pipeline opportunity identified
   - [ ] First co-marketing campaign launched
   - [ ] Partner has independently completed a demo or pitch
   - **Gate:** Partner has generated at least one qualified opportunity

8. **Handoff to Ongoing Management**
   - Schedule first QBR (90 days from activation)
   - Transfer from onboarding project to ongoing partner scorecard
   - Document lessons learned for onboarding process improvement

### Workflow 2: MDF Program Management

Manage marketing development fund allocation and accountability.

1. **Budget Allocation** (Finn)
   - Define total MDF pool by quarter
   - Allocate by partner tier (e.g., Strategic: $X, Growth: $Y)
   - Set per-partner caps and rollover rules

2. **Request Process**
   - Partner submits MDF request (campaign type, budget, expected outcomes)
   - Partner Manager reviews alignment with joint goals
   - Finance approves budget
   - SLA: 5 business days from submission to decision

3. **Execution Support** (Mia + Cora)
   - Provide campaign templates and co-branded assets
   - Review partner-created content for brand compliance
   - Coordinate joint execution where applicable

4. **Proof of Execution**
   - Partner submits proof (screenshots, reports, invoices)
   - Partner Manager verifies completion
   - Finance processes reimbursement
   - SLA: 15 business days from proof submission to payment

5. **ROI Tracking**
   - Track leads, pipeline, and revenue attributed to MDF campaigns
   - Report ROI by partner, campaign type, and quarter
   - Use results to inform next quarter's allocation

### Workflow 3: Partner Enablement Sprint

Rapid enablement for a new product launch or market expansion.

1. Identify what's new (product update, new market, new integration)
2. Assess partner readiness (who needs what training?)
3. Create enablement package (Cora):
   - Updated pitch deck (what's changed, why it matters)
   - New battle card or competitive update
   - Customer-facing one-pager
   - FAQ document
4. Deliver training:
   - Live webinar for all partners (recorded)
   - Hands-on workshop for top-tier partners
   - Self-serve learning module for long tail
5. Certify: quiz or demo sign-off for partners handling the new capability
6. Launch: coordinated announcement with partner co-promotion

### Workflow 4: Partner Health Check & Re-engagement

For partners showing low activity or declining performance.

1. Pull partner scorecard data:
   - Pipeline generated (last 90 days)
   - Deals closed (last 90 days)
   - Training completion
   - Engagement (portal logins, event attendance, content downloads)
2. Classify: Active / At-risk / Inactive
3. For At-risk partners:
   - Partner Manager outreach (understand blockers)
   - Refresher training offer
   - Joint campaign incentive
   - 30-day re-engagement plan with specific milestones
4. For Inactive partners (180+ days no activity):
   - Formal re-engagement proposal with 60-day deadline
   - If no response or commitment: begin offboarding (revoke access, archive records)
5. Document and report findings to partner program leadership
