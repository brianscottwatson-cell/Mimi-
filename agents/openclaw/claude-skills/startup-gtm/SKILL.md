# Startup GTM Skill

Plan and execute go-to-market strategies for early-stage products and new market entries.

---

## Why

Most startups fail not because the product is bad, but because they never find repeatable distribution. This skill exists to compress the time from "we built something" to "people are buying it" by applying structured GTM thinking — positioning, channel selection, messaging, and launch sequencing — before spending money on ads or content.

**Core belief:** GTM is not marketing. It is the alignment of product, audience, channel, and timing into a repeatable motion.

---

## Map

### Inputs
- Product / service description (what it does, who it's for)
- Current stage (pre-launch, soft launch, post-launch pivot)
- Budget constraints (bootstrap, seed-funded, Series A+)
- Competitive landscape (known alternatives, substitutes)
- Existing assets (landing page, waitlist, email list, social presence)

### Outputs
- **Positioning statement** — one sentence that passes the "so what?" test
- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — firmographic + psychographic + behavioral
- **Channel map** — ranked channels by expected CAC and time-to-signal
- **Messaging matrix** — by persona × stage × channel
- **Launch plan** — phased timeline with milestones and owner assignments
- **Metrics dashboard spec** — leading indicators, lagging indicators, north star

## Rules

1. **No launch without positioning.** Positioning must be defined and validated before any campaign work begins.
2. **ICP before channel.** Channel selection is a function of where the ICP already spends attention, not where the founder wants to be.
3. **One channel to start.** Prove one channel works before diversifying. Spreading thin kills startups.
4. **Message-market fit before product-market fit.** If you can't describe the value in one sentence that makes the buyer lean in, the product isn't ready for GTM.
5. **Budget reality.** All recommendations must be executable within stated budget. No aspirational plans.
6. **Time-boxed experiments.** Every growth experiment must have a hypothesis, a run time (max 2 weeks), and a kill criterion.
7. **Founder-led selling first.** For B2B, the first 10-20 customers should come from founder outreach, not paid ads.

## Workflows

### Workflow 1: Full GTM Plan

Use when starting from scratch or pivoting.

1. **Discovery** — Gather product details, stage, budget, competitive context
2. **Research** (Rex) — Market sizing, competitor positioning, keyword landscape, audience behavior
3. **Positioning** (Vale) — Draft positioning statement, test against "so what?" and "compared to what?" filters
4. **ICP Definition** (Vale + Rex) — Build ICP with firmographic, psychographic, and behavioral attributes
5. **Channel Ranking** (Mia) — Score channels by CAC estimate, time-to-signal, and ICP overlap
6. **Messaging** (Cora) — Create messaging matrix: persona × funnel stage × channel
7. **Unit Economics** (Finn) — Model CAC, LTV, payback period; set budget guardrails
8. **Launch Plan** (Pax) — Phase timeline (Week 1-2: foundation, Week 3-4: soft launch, Week 5-8: scale what works)
9. **Metrics** — Define north star metric, leading indicators, weekly review cadence
10. **Review checkpoint** — Present full plan for human approval before execution

### Workflow 2: Positioning Workshop

Use when the product exists but messaging isn't landing.

1. Collect current positioning attempts and customer feedback
2. Run "Five Whys" on the value proposition
3. Map competitive alternatives (including "do nothing")
4. Draft 3 positioning candidates using the format: `For [ICP] who [situation], [product] is the [category] that [key benefit] unlike [alternative] because [reason to believe]`
5. Score each on clarity, differentiation, and believability
6. Select winner, create derivative taglines and elevator pitches

### Workflow 3: Channel Experiment Sprint

Use when you need to validate a new acquisition channel quickly.

1. Select channel based on ICP overlap score
2. Define hypothesis: "We believe [channel] will deliver [X] qualified leads at [Y] CAC within [Z] days"
3. Design minimum viable campaign (Mia + Cora)
4. Set budget cap and kill criterion
5. Launch, measure daily
6. At end of sprint: decide to scale, iterate, or kill
7. Document learnings regardless of outcome
