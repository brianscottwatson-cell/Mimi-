# Design Philosophies — Inspiration Guide

## 1. Neo-Artisanat Digital
**Essence:** Warmth of traditional craftsmanship meets clean digital presentation.
- **Colors:** Warm earth tones (terracotta, warm gray, cream) with one vibrant accent
- **Typography:** Serif headings (DM Serif Display) + clean sans body (Inter)
- **Layout:** Generous whitespace, asymmetric grids, full-bleed images
- **Signature elements:** Hand-drawn accents, textured backgrounds, rounded shapes
- **Animation:** Slow, organic reveals (fade-up, parallax)
- **Best for:** Artisans, renovation, traditional trades, premium services

## 2. Brutalist Confidence
**Essence:** Bold, no-nonsense design that commands attention and trust.
- **Colors:** High contrast — near-black + white + one neon accent (electric blue or red)
- **Typography:** Bold grotesque headings (Space Grotesk, Instrument Sans) + monospace accents
- **Layout:** Large type, stark sections, heavy borders, intentional roughness
- **Signature elements:** Oversized headings, visible grid, raw edges
- **Animation:** Snappy, mechanical (slide-in, hard cuts)
- **Best for:** Emergency services, construction, industrial, tech-forward trades

## 3. Soft Modernism
**Essence:** Approachable, clean, and trustworthy — the "friendly professional."
- **Colors:** Soft palette — slate blue, light gray, white, with warm accent (coral or gold)
- **Typography:** Rounded sans (Nunito, Poppins) for everything — friendly and readable
- **Layout:** Card-based, centered, generous padding, soft shadows
- **Signature elements:** Rounded corners, subtle gradients, icon illustrations
- **Animation:** Gentle (fade, scale-up on scroll)
- **Best for:** Healthcare, cleaning, childcare, personal services

## 4. Vibrant Energy
**Essence:** Dynamic, colorful, energetic — stands out in crowded markets.
- **Colors:** Bold primaries (electric blue + bright orange/yellow) on dark or white base
- **Typography:** Geometric sans (Montserrat, Outfit) — strong and modern
- **Layout:** Dynamic angles, overlapping elements, zigzag sections
- **Signature elements:** Diagonal cuts, gradient overlays, badge/stamp motifs
- **Animation:** Energetic (bounce, slide, rotate)
- **Best for:** Landscaping, moving, fitness, youth-oriented services

## 5. Luxury Minimalism
**Essence:** Restrained elegance that signals premium quality and exclusivity.
- **Colors:** Monochrome (charcoal, off-white) + one metallic accent (gold or copper)
- **Typography:** Thin serif headings (Playfair Display, Cormorant) + light sans body
- **Layout:** Extreme whitespace, centered compositions, editorial feel
- **Signature elements:** Fine lines, letter-spacing, subtle hover states
- **Animation:** Barely there — slow fades, gentle parallax
- **Best for:** Architecture, interior design, high-end renovation, consulting

---

## Selection Criteria

When choosing a design philosophy, consider:
1. **Target audience demographics** — age, income, expectations
2. **Industry norms** — what competitors look like (match or differentiate?)
3. **Conversion priority** — flashy designs can distract from CTAs
4. **Content volume** — minimal content suits minimalism; heavy content needs structure
5. **Brand personality** — does the business feel warm? Bold? Premium? Friendly?
