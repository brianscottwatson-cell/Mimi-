# Startup Growth Framework

## Growth Stage Definitions

### Stage 0: Idea → Prototype
- **Primary Goal**: Build something people want
- **Key Metrics**: Customer interviews completed, prototype feedback scores
- **Marketing Focus**: Customer development, problem validation
- **Growth Tactics**: Direct customer outreach, problem interviews, solution validation

### Stage 1: Prototype → Product-Market Fit
- **Primary Goal**: Find repeatable, scalable growth
- **Key Metrics**: Retention curves, NPS >50, organic growth signals
- **Marketing Focus**: Message-market fit, channel-product fit testing
- **Growth Tactics**: Content marketing, founder-led sales, early adopter programs

### Stage 2: PMF → Scale
- **Primary Goal**: Build predictable growth machine
- **Key Metrics**: CAC payback <12 months, LTV:CAC >3:1, sustained growth rate >20% MoM
- **Marketing Focus**: Channel optimization, automation, team building
- **Growth Tactics**: Paid acquisition, referral programs, partnership development

## Product-Market Fit Measurement Framework

### The PMF Score Calculation

**Formula**: PMF Score = (Retention × 0.4) + (Satisfaction × 0.3) + (Market Response × 0.2) + (Growth Efficiency × 0.1)

**Component Definitions**:

1. **Retention Score (0-10)**
   - 10: >60% of users active after 3 months
   - 8: 40-60% active after 3 months  
   - 6: 20-40% active after 3 months
   - 4: 10-20% active after 3 months
   - 2: <10% active after 3 months

2. **Satisfaction Score (0-10)**
   - 10: >40% "very disappointed" if product went away
   - 8: 25-40% very disappointed
   - 6: 15-25% very disappointed
   - 4: 5-15% very disappointed
   - 2: <5% very disappointed

3. **Market Response Score (0-10)**
   - 10: Strong organic growth, word-of-mouth, press coverage
   - 8: Some organic signals, occasional mentions
   - 6: Neutral market response, no strong signals
   - 4: Market response requires heavy pushing
   - 2: Market actively resistant to product

4. **Growth Efficiency Score (0-10)**
   - 10: CAC decreasing over time, viral coefficient >1
   - 8: CAC stable, some organic amplification
   - 6: CAC stable, linear growth
   - 4: CAC increasing, growth requires more input
   - 2: CAC increasing rapidly, unsustainable economics

**Interpretation**:
- **8-10**: Strong PMF - Focus on scaling
- **6-8**: Moderate PMF - Optimize before major scaling
- **4-6**: Weak PMF - Significant product/market work needed  
- **0-4**: No PMF - Pivot or major iteration required

## Customer Development Process

### Interview Framework

**Pre-Interview Setup**:
- Target: 15-20 interviews across customer segments
- Duration: 30-45 minutes each
- Method: Video call with recording (permission required)
- Incentive: Product credits, gift card, or exclusive access

**Interview Structure** (45 minutes):

*Opening (5 minutes)*:
- Thank them, explain purpose, get recording permission
- "I'm trying to understand how people like you approach [problem area]"

*Problem Discovery (15 minutes)*:
- "Tell me about the last time you encountered [problem your product solves]"
- "What was frustrating about that experience?"
- "How do you currently handle this?"
- "What would have to happen for this to become a bigger priority?"

*Solution Exploration (15 minutes)*:
- "Show me how you currently solve this" (screen share if applicable)
- "What tools/methods do you use?"
- "What do you like/dislike about your current approach?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the perfect solution do?"

*Product Feedback (8 minutes)*:
- "I'm going to show you what we're building" [demo core features]
- "What's your reaction to this?"
- "How does this compare to what you're doing now?"
- "What concerns would you have about switching?"

*Wrap-up (2 minutes)*:
- "Would you be interested in trying this when it's ready?"
- "Who else do you know that has this problem?"
- "Any final thoughts or questions?"

### Customer Insight Synthesis

**Pattern Identification**:
1. **Job-to-be-Done Analysis**: What job is the customer really hiring your product to do?
2. **Pain Point Intensity**: Which problems cause the most frustration/cost?
3. **Current Solution Analysis**: What do customers do now? What do they pay?
4. **Decision Criteria**: What factors determine if they'll switch solutions?
5. **Success Metrics**: How do customers measure success in this area?

**Customer Language Mapping**:
- Record exact phrases customers use to describe their problem
- Note emotional words ("frustrating", "critical", "impossible")
- Identify industry jargon vs. plain language
- Map feature names customers use vs. what you call them

**Persona Development** (Based on Behavior, Not Demographics):

**Primary Persona Template**:
- **Name**: [Descriptive name based on behavior]
- **Core Job**: What they're trying to accomplish
- **Main Frustrations**: Top 3 pain points with current solutions
- **Success Criteria**: How they measure success
- **Decision Process**: How they evaluate and buy solutions
- **Preferred Channels**: Where they look for information/solutions
- **Key Messaging**: Language that resonates with this persona

## Growth Experiment Design Framework

### Experiment Prioritization Matrix

**ICE Scoring** (Impact × Confidence × Ease, each rated 1-10):

- **Impact**: How much could this move your key metric?
  - 10: Could improve key metric by >25%
  - 5: Could improve key metric by 10-25%  
  - 1: Would improve key metric by <5%

- **Confidence**: How sure are you this will work?
  - 10: Strong evidence this approach works
  - 5: Some evidence, reasonable hypothesis
  - 1: Pure speculation, no supporting evidence

- **Ease**: How difficult is this to implement?
  - 10: Can implement in <1 week with existing resources
  - 5: Requires 2-4 weeks and some new resources
  - 1: Requires >1 month and significant resources

**Total ICE Score**: Impact × Confidence × Ease ÷ 1000

Focus on experiments with ICE scores >5.0 first.

### Experiment Documentation Template

```
**Experiment Name**: [Descriptive name]
**Hypothesis**: If we [change], then [metric] will [change by X] because [reason]
**Test Type**: A/B test | Landing page test | Cohort analysis | Other
**Primary Metric**: [Key success metric]
**Secondary Metrics**: [Learning metrics]
**Sample Size Required**: [Number of users/conversions needed]
**Test Duration**: [Timeline]
**Traffic Allocation**: [% to each variant]
**Success Criteria**: [Specific threshold for success/failure]
```

### Statistical Significance Guidelines

**Minimum Sample Sizes** (for 95% confidence, 80% power):

|Current Conversion Rate|Minimum Detectable Effect|Sample Size Per Variant|
|-----------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
|1%                     |50% relative improvement |3,200                   |
|5%                     |20% relative improvement |1,600                   |
|10%                    |15% relative improvement |2,200                   |
|25%                    |10% relative improvement |3,800                   |

**Test Duration Guidelines**:
- Minimum: 1 week (to account for day-of-week effects)
- Maximum: 4 weeks (longer tests lose relevance)
- Business cycle: Run through complete purchase cycle if >1 week

## Content Marketing for Early-Stage Startups

### Content-Channel Fit Matrix

|Content Type       |Primary Channel    |Secondary Channel|Time Investment|Lead Quality|
|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|------------|
|Problem-focused blog|SEO, LinkedIn     |Twitter, Reddit  |Medium         |High        |
|Solution tutorials |YouTube, blog     |LinkedIn, email  |High           |Very High   |
|Industry insights  |LinkedIn, Twitter |Newsletter, blog |Medium         |Medium      |
|Customer stories   |Website, LinkedIn |Email, PR        |Low            |High        |
|Product demos      |Website, YouTube  |Social, email    |Medium         |Very High   |

### Content Calendar Framework

**Monthly Content Mix** (for 16 pieces/month):
- 40% Educational (problems, tutorials, how-tos)
- 25% Industry insights (trends, analysis, commentary)  
- 20% Product-related (features, use cases, demos)
- 15% Social proof (customer stories, testimonials, case studies)

**Distribution Strategy**:
- Publish on owned channel first (blog, YouTube)
- Adapt for social channels (LinkedIn, Twitter threads)
- Repurpose into email newsletter content
- Create social snippets for ongoing distribution

### Channel Performance Tracking

**For Each Marketing Channel, Track**:
- Traffic/Impressions generated
- Cost per click/engagement  
- Conversion rate to signup
- Cost per acquisition
- Customer quality (activation, retention, LTV)
- Time to payback

**Channel Health Scorecard**:
- Green: CAC < 1/3 LTV, >30% of customers activate
- Yellow: CAC < 1/2 LTV, 20-30% activation rate
- Red: CAC > 1/2 LTV or <20% activation rate
